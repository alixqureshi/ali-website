The Internet Is Going Crazy Over Popcorn That Tastes Like a Cinnamon Bun (And 7 Other Flavors That Shouldn't Exist But Absolutely Do)

A popcorn company figured out how to bake flavor INTO each kernel before it pops. We tried all of them. Some are borderline illegal.

Look, I wasn't expecting to write 1,200 words about popcorn today. But then someone in our Slack dropped a link to a company that wraps each individual kernel in flavor before it pops, and I lost about 45 minutes of my life reading reviews and watching TikToks.

The company is called Opopop. They were founded by the guys who created Beatport (the music platform), which is already a weird origin story for a popcorn brand. They've since raised $12 million in funding, landed in the Oscars gift bags, and have investor backing from the guy who built RXBAR and, inexplicably, Tiësto.

Their whole thing: "Flavor Wrapped Kernels." Instead of dusting seasoning on top of popcorn after it's popped (where it mostly falls to the bottom of the bag), they coat each kernel in flavoring first. So when it pops, the flavor is literally baked in.

I ordered the variety pack. Here's every flavor, ranked by how likely I am to hide the bag from my roommates.

"So good, you'll be mad at every other popcorn you've ever eaten." — Opopop's actual tagline, and honestly? They're not wrong.

"Because popcorn in the AM is totally a thing."

If you buy one flavor, make it this one. It's the gateway drug.

How It Actually Works

The "Peel, Pour, Pop" System

  • Peel — Open a sachet of flavor-wrapped kernels
  • Pour — Dump them into the reusable silicone popper (or a microwave bag if you prefer)
  • Pop — Microwave 2-3 minutes. Done. No oil, no stovetop, no cleanup beyond rinsing the popper

The reusable popper is a nice touch — it's dishwasher safe and means you're not throwing away a bag every time. One big bag fills the popper about 4 times.

What surprised me most: almost zero unpopped kernels. With regular microwave popcorn I'm used to a graveyard of duds at the bottom. With Opopop, I counted maybe 3-4 stragglers per batch. Their "popcorn scientists" (real title, apparently) seem to have figured out kernel quality.

The Catch

It's not cheap. A big bag runs $17.99 — roughly 4x what you'd pay for a box of Orville at the grocery store. The Discovery Kit (7 flavors + popper) is $44.99.

But here's the reframe: a bag of Fancy Butter makes about 36 cups of popcorn. That's roughly $0.50 per bowl. You're paying more than grocery store popcorn, but less than a single bag of chips — for something that actually tastes like the flavor on the label.

If you're buying this as a gift? The variety pack at $45.99 is the move. Twelve bags, every flavor. It looks impressive, it's actually interesting, and the person won't just shove it in a drawer like another candle.
